Dr. B. R. Ambedkar was a great economist, jurist, social reformer, and the chief architect of the Indian Constitution. His life is an inspiring example of courage, determination, and the pursuit of knowledge.

Ambedkar strongly opposed the caste system because he believed that it denied basic human rights and dignity to millions of people. He argued that caste was not only a division of labour but a division of labourers, which led to inequality and injustice.

He understood that meaningful social change requires both legal reforms and a change in mindset. Therefore, he worked tirelessly to promote education, equality, and social justice. His fight against discrimination was rooted in his belief that all humans are equal and deserve respect.

Ambedkar believed that political democracy cannot succeed without social democracy. He defined social democracy as a way of life that recognizes liberty, equality, and fraternity as essential principles. According to him, without equality, liberty would be meaningless, and without fraternity, equality would not last.

He also emphasised the importance of thinking critically and questioning blind traditions. Ambedkar believed that progress is possible only when people develop a scientific temper and abandon outdated beliefs.
